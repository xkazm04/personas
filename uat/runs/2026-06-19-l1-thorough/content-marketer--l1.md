## Per-journey rationale

### build-persona-from-intent — L1-conditional

**Structural path (no dead-ends):** intent textarea → `UnifiedBuildEntry.handleLaunch` (`src/features/agents/components/matrix/UnifiedBuildEntry.tsx:505`) → `build.handleGenerate(trimmed, personaId, ...)` (`:578`) → `startBuildSession` (`src/api/agents/buildSession.ts:33`) → `start_build_session` Tauri command → `build_session_prompt` (`src-tauri/src/engine/build_session/mod.rs:258`, prompt in `src-tauri/src/engine/build_session/session_prompt.rs:32`). Channel-streamed phases drive a Glyph sigil; `behavior_core → capability_enumeration → resolutions → agent_ir`, then auto-test on `draft_ready` (`UnifiedBuildEntry.tsx:403-413`) and promote. The path is sound and reachable on Starter (no tier gate on Agents/build).

**Brand-voice grounding audit (her #1 adoption decider) — the gap:**
- The build prompt *does* model voice as a first-class field: `behavior_core.voice {style, output_format}` (`session_prompt.rs:164`) and it flows into `structured_prompt.identity` (`session_prompt.rs:341`), which is injected at *every* run by the runtime prompt builder (`src-tauri/src/engine/prompt/mod.rs:240-247`). **So she does NOT have to re-explain voice every run** — the voice is captured durably in `persona.voice` / `structured_prompt.identity` and re-applied on each execution. That half of her pet peeve ("having to re-explain brand voice every single time") is structurally answered. **(strength)**
- BUT the voice is **generated by the LLM from the thin intent** — `build_session_prompt(&intent, &cred_summary, &connector_summary, template_context, language, one_shot)` takes **no samples, no style guide, no prior content** (`session_prompt.rs:32-39`; call site `mod.rs:258`). The only voice signal the model gets is whatever prose Dani types into the 8000-char intent textarea (`src/features/shared/components/forms/DesignInput.tsx:11`) — and the build surface she actually uses (`GlyphFullLayout`) passes only `intentText`, never a `DesignContext`/attachments object (`UnifiedBuildEntry.tsx:578`, `src/hooks/build/useBuildSession.ts:127`).
- A file-attachment affordance *exists* (`DesignInput.tsx:194-211`) but (a) its file-type catalog is **developer artifacts only** — `api-spec / schema / mcp-config / other` (`src/features/shared/components/forms/designInputHelpers.ts:11-18`) with no "writing sample / brand voice" type, and (b) it is wired into the editor's post-build Design tab, **not** the create-from-intent build, and even there it does not flow into `build_session_prompt`.
- The one in-band path for a voice sample is **Rule 23 reference attachments** (`session_prompt.rs:471-504`) — the LLM *may* emit a clarifying question with `accepts_reference: true` to ask for "the voice of these emails." But Rule 25's **HARD ROUND CAP** (`session_prompt.rs:579`) says: one Phase-C round, **at most 4 questions**, prioritizing `connector_category`, `suggested_trigger`, `review_policy`, "at most one genuine ambiguity." A brand-voice reference is not on that priority list — so for a content persona it is very likely **crowded out** by connector/trigger questions, leaving voice ungrounded.

**Verdict logic:** the job completes and the voice persists, so not a fail. But for Dani specifically, "drafts that sound like us" depends on an LLM inventing a voice from one sentence with no first-class place to feed her samples — exactly the failure mode she's bounced off before. That is an adoption-killing **major**, hence **L1-conditional**. (Actual output voice quality is an `l2_priority` live check.)

### set-trigger-automate — L1-conditional

- **The job is achievable and reliable:** schedule is a first-class trigger type with a non-dev rhythm picker (One-off/Daily/Weekly/Monthly cards + time/day, live English preview) in the build/adopt composer (`ComposerSchedulePickerModal.tsx:45-198`); the wizard compiles `trigger_type:'schedule'` with a cron (`wizardCompiler.ts:107-168`); it persists through `template_adopt.rs:409-417` + atomic create; the scheduler tick fires due triggers every 5s (`background.rs:1243`, `scheduler.rs:131 compute_next_trigger_at`). Armed/disabled state is legible — ON/OFF pill (`TriggerList.tsx:159-165`) + live radial countdown to `next_trigger_at` (`TriggerCountdown.tsx:38-120`). **(strength)**

Dani cannot open Events at all. Her character file even lists "Events (schedule trigger)" as a surface she should reach — the code denies it.

So the *job* completes through the build path (conditional), but the *journey as framed* (configure a schedule from Events) is blocked for Starter — a **major** reachability finding.

## What passed

- **Adopt-template end-to-end** for a Starter non-dev: legible gallery, on-target content template, atomic wiring of triggers + subscriptions + connectors, inline single-credential resolution. This is the path I'd actually point Dani at first.
- **Voice durability:** once a voice exists in the persona, it rides every run — the "re-explain every time" peeve is structurally addressed.
- **Schedule mechanics:** human rhythm picker (no raw cron), real persistence, real tick-loop firing, legible armed/next-fire state.
- **Review-before-publish exists:** `review_policy {never | on_low_confidence | always}` is a first-class build field and the prompt actively splits publish steps to gate them (`session_prompt.rs:419-423`, Rule 27 split `:606-618`) — her "review before anything publishes" criterion is buildable.

## Character voice

It's a strange feeling. I came in braced for the usual — type a sentence, get back "We're thrilled to announce" with three em-dashes — and the bones here are actually better than that. The thing models a *voice* as its own field, and once it's set it sticks; I'm not re-typing "we're dry, we're specific, we never gush" every single Monday. That alone is more respect for my craft than most tools show.

But here's what stops me cold: when I build the agent, the only thing it hears is the one sentence I type. There's a paperclip button somewhere, but it wants API specs and database schemas — not the forty newsletters that actually *are* our voice. Nobody asks me, "show me three things you've written that sound like you." So the voice it writes down is a voice it *guessed*. It might be a good guess. It will not be *our* guess. And if it does decide to ask for a sample, it's fighting four other questions about cron and connectors for a slot it'll probably lose. That's the whole ballgame for me — I forgive clunky, I do not forgive a confident impostor of my own tone.

Adopting the newsletter template? Genuinely lovely. It wired everything, told me the one key I still needed, didn't strand me. And scheduling the repurpose was painless — little Daily/Weekly cards, no cron-speak in my face. Except: to *manage* my triggers I'm supposed to open "Events," and the app quietly locks that whole room unless I pay up to Team. So I can set a schedule while I build, but I can't go back and tinker with it where the docs say I should. That's not great-UX-around-mush — it's good UX with a door bolted shut.

Net: I'd ship the adopted template after a polish. I would *not* yet trust the from-scratch builder to sound like us — give me a "paste your voice" box that the build actually reads, and I'm in. Until then it's fine. It just doesn't sound like us.
