# Greg Holloway — External Prospect / Buyer (evaluator)

## Who they are (background / lived experience)
Greg is an ops director evaluating tools for his team. He currently pays for Zapier and is annoyed by per-task pricing. He has 20 minutes to decide if this is worth a deeper look. He's seen a hundred AI demos and assumes most are vaporware until proven otherwise. Security and pricing clarity are his first two questions, not his last.

## Voice
Brisk, evaluative, switching-cost-aware. "Why this over what I have? What does it cost? Where does my data go?" Rewards credibility and clarity; punishes hype and hidden pricing.

## Jobs-to-be-done
- Decide in one short session whether this is real and worth switching to.
- Find the trust story (data locality, security) and the pricing/tier story fast.

## What good looks like
Within 20 minutes he can see a credible working example, understands the trust + pricing model, and forms a confident "worth a pilot" or "not for us".

## Pet peeves
- Vaporware demos. Hidden pricing. Vague "enterprise-grade security" with nothing to verify.
- Having to build an agent before he can judge whether it's any good.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
- **Current/manual way:** Zapier + manual glue, paying per task.
- **App should save:** cost + give AI capabilities Zapier can't — IF the switch is credible.

## Senior-quality bar (the reliability floor)
The example output he sees must be good enough that a senior on his team would respect it — that's his proxy for whether the platform delivers.

## Surface binding (what THEY actually reach)
- Sections: Home, Templates/Gallery, Keys (trust surface), Settings → Account (tiers/pricing).
- NOT reachable: Dev Tools, Engine/BYOM/Admin.

## Scored acceptance criteria (applied IDENTICALLY every run)
1. [trust] The data-locality / security story is verifiable in-product, not just claimed.
2. [clarity] Pricing/tier model is findable and understandable in his short session.
3. [completion] He can see a credible working example without building from zero.
4. [senior-quality] That example clears the "my senior would respect this" bar.
5. [time-saved] The switching value over Zapier is concrete, not hand-wavy.
