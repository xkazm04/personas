# Dani Okafor — Content Marketer

## Who they are (background / lived experience)
Dani owns content for a 30-person B2B startup. Writes the newsletter, repurposes it into social, keeps a content calendar. Has tried AI writing tools and bounced off most because the output sounded like everyone else's — generic LinkedIn voice. Brand voice is their craft identity; bland output is an insult, not a time-saver.

## Voice
Warm, opinionated about words. "It's fine, but it doesn't sound like us." Will forgive a clunky UI for output that nails the voice; won't forgive great UX wrapped around mush.

## Jobs-to-be-done
- Repurpose one newsletter into 5 on-brand social posts on a schedule.
- Keep a consistent voice without writing every piece from scratch.

## What good looks like
Drafts they'd ship with light edits, in the company's actual voice, scheduled to post without babysitting.

## Pet peeves
- Generic AI voice. Em-dash-laden "thrilled to announce" slop.
- Having to re-explain brand voice every single time.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
- **Current/manual way:** newsletter → social repurposing ~2 hrs/issue.
- **App should save:** down to ~20 min of editing if the voice lands.

## Senior-quality bar (the reliability floor)
Copy a senior content marketer would ship after a quick polish — specific, on-brand, no clichés, correct product facts.

## Surface binding (what THEY actually reach)
- Sections: Personas, Templates, Events (schedule trigger), Overview.
- NOT reachable: Dev Tools, Engine/BYOM/Admin, Teams.

## Scored acceptance criteria (applied IDENTICALLY every run)
1. [senior-quality] Output is on-brand enough to ship with light edits (the make-or-break check).
2. [grounding/missing] The build/run actually ingests brand-voice context, not just the thin intent.
3. [completion] She can put repurposing on a recurring schedule without dev help.
4. [time-saved] Net faster than writing the posts herself.
5. [trust] She can review before anything publishes.
