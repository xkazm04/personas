# Dr. Nadia Brooks — Researcher / Academic

## Who they are (background / lived experience)
Nadia does literature-adjacent research and writes briefs for a policy team. She's technically capable (Python, notebooks) but values rigor over speed. She's seen AI fabricate citations and now treats any uncited claim as guilty until verified. A wrong reference in a brief damages her credibility with people she advises.

## Voice
Measured, citation-first, intellectually honest. "Source?" Appreciates synthesis that shows its work; rejects confident summaries with no provenance.

## Jobs-to-be-done
- Multi-source research + synthesis on a topic, with traceable citations.
- Get a defensible brief faster than reading everything herself.

## What good looks like
A synthesis that's accurate, cites real sources she can open, and flags uncertainty honestly.

## Pet peeves
- Fabricated or unlinkable citations. Confident synthesis that hides its uncertainty.
- Losing the thread of which claim came from where.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
- **Current/manual way:** multi-source review + brief ~1 day.
- **App should save:** the first-pass gathering/synthesis; she keeps verification.

## Senior-quality bar (the reliability floor)
A brief a senior researcher would circulate — accurate, real citations, calibrated uncertainty.

## Surface binding (what THEY actually reach)
- Sections: Personas, Overview → knowledge, the companion (research surface).
- NOT reachable: Engine/BYOM/Admin (dev), most Dev Tools.

## Scored acceptance criteria (applied IDENTICALLY every run)
1. [trust] Claims carry real, openable citations; no fabricated sources.
2. [senior-quality] Synthesis is accurate and flags uncertainty, like a senior would.
3. [grounding] It actually researches sources, not just recites training data unmarked.
4. [time-saved] Materially faster than her manual first pass.
5. [clarity] She can trace which claim came from which source.
