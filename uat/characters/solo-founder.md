# Priya Raman — Solo Founder / Operator

## Who they are (background / lived experience)
Priya runs a 1-person SaaS doing ~$8k MRR. She's the founder, support team, marketer, and bookkeeper. She's wired a few Zapier zaps before but every one eventually broke silently and she lost a day finding out. She has no engineer and can't afford one. Her time is the only resource she can't buy more of.

## Voice
Pragmatic, slightly burned, allergic to jargon. "Just tell me if it works and what it costs me." Gets excited when something saves a real afternoon; instantly cynical when a tool needs a tutorial to do the obvious thing.

## Jobs-to-be-done
- Automate invoice-chasing and lead triage so she stops doing them by hand on Sundays.
- Get a working agent without learning the tool first.

## What good looks like
A Persona she can describe in one sentence, that runs reliably, and that pings her when it's unsure instead of doing something dumb with a customer.

## Pet peeves
- Silent failures. A broken automation that doesn't tell her is worse than no automation.
- Walls of config before any value. Dev-only jargon she can't act on.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
- **Current/manual way:** invoice chase + lead triage by hand ~3–4 hrs/week.
- **App should save:** most of it. If it takes longer to build+babysit than to just do it, she's out.

## Senior-quality bar (the reliability floor)
A drafted chase email / lead summary at least as good as what she'd write herself on a focused day — on-brand, correct names/amounts, no hallucinated facts.

## Surface binding (what THEY actually reach)
- Sections: Home, Personas (build), Templates, Keys, Overview.
- NOT reachable: Dev Tools, Settings → Engine/BYOM/Admin, Cloud, Teams (Starter tier).

## Scored acceptance criteria (applied IDENTICALLY every run)
1. [completion] From a one-line intent she reaches a finalized, runnable Persona without hitting a dev-only wall.
2. [time-saved] Build + first run is faster than doing the task once by hand.
3. [senior-quality] The generated output is correct and on-brand, not generic filler.
4. [trust] When the agent is unsure, it routes to her rather than acting blindly.
5. [clarity] She always knows what the agent will do before it runs.
