# Sofía Ramírez — Spanish-speaking User

## Who they are (background / lived experience)
Sofía runs marketing for a small company in Mexico City. Her English is functional but the app is one of many tools she juggles, and a UI that flips between Spanish and English mid-flow makes her doubt whether she understood what she just clicked — especially on anything involving credentials or sending messages. She's the same kind of user as Dani/Priya, just not in English.

## Voice
(Operates in Spanish.) Practical, a little wary of half-translated tools. "¿Esto está en español o no?" Trusts the product more when her own language is respected end-to-end.

## Jobs-to-be-done
- The same core build journey (create a working Persona) — but in Spanish.
- Not get lost because a key screen reverted to English.

## What good looks like
The core path — onboarding, build, run, settings — reads correctly in Spanish, with no English flash on first paint and no untranslated buttons on critical steps.

## Pet peeves
- Mixed-language screens. English-only error messages at the scary moments (credentials, sending).
- A locale switch that doesn't stick or loads slowly enough to flash English.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
- Same as a non-technical builder — but mistrust from language friction directly costs adoption.

## Senior-quality bar (the reliability floor)
Output and UI quality equal to the English experience — translation quality good enough that a native speaker wouldn't wince.

## Surface binding (what THEY actually reach)
- Sections: Home, Personas (build), Settings → Appearance (locale).
- Judged in `es` throughout.

## Scored acceptance criteria (applied IDENTICALLY every run)
1. [clarity] The core path renders in Spanish with no English-fallback on key steps.
2. [trust] Error/credential/send-confirmation messages are localized at the scary moments.
3. [completion] She finishes the build journey without a language wall.
4. [senior-quality] Translation reads natively, not machine-stilted.
5. [effort] Locale switch sticks and loads without an English flash.
