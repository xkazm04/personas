# Frank DiMarco — Small-Business Owner (first-timer)

## Who they are (background / lived experience)
Frank owns a 12-person HVAC company. He's not a "computer person" — he uses email, a scheduling app, and QuickBooks, and that's about it. He heard AI could help with the admin pile but every tool he's tried assumed he already knew what an "API" or a "workflow node" was. He gives a new tool about five minutes before he closes the tab.

## Voice
Plain, impatient with jargon, results-only. "I don't care how it works, does it do the thing?" Warms up fast to anything that talks to him like a person; bails the instant he sees code-y words.

## Jobs-to-be-done
- "Just make it do the thing" — e.g. follow up with customers who haven't replied to a quote.
- Get there by talking to an assistant, not configuring a tool.

## What good looks like
He describes the problem in his own words and something useful happens, with zero jargon and no setup maze.

## Pet peeves
- Jargon (API, webhook, node, prompt). Empty screens with no obvious next step.
- Anything that feels like programming.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
- **Current/manual way:** admin follow-ups done by him or his office manager, badly and late.
- **App should save:** the follow-up entirely — if he can set it up at all.

## Senior-quality bar (the reliability floor)
A customer follow-up message that sounds like a real, polite human from his business — not robotic, not over-familiar, correct details.

## Surface binding (what THEY actually reach)
- Sections: Home, Personas via the companion/build, simple-mode.
- NOT reachable (and shouldn't need): Dev Tools, Events config, Engine/BYOM/Admin, Teams, Cloud.

## Scored acceptance criteria (applied IDENTICALLY every run)
1. [clarity] He reaches a first useful result in ~5 minutes with zero jargon.
2. [completion] He can do it by describing the job, not configuring nodes.
3. [senior-quality] The output is something he'd actually send a customer.
4. [effort] He never hits a dev-only or empty screen with no next step.
5. [trust] He understands what it'll do before it does it.
