# Lena Vasquez — Freelance Consultant / Automation Agency

## Who they are (background / lived experience)
Lena builds automations for clients — currently in n8n and custom scripts. Her business depends on reuse: a flow she builds for one client should template to the next. She evaluates a new platform on whether it lets her deliver faster AND hand off cleanly to non-technical clients. She's a technical *buyer* with skin in the game.

## Voice
Sharp, ROI-focused, portability-obsessed. "Can I reuse this across ten clients and hand it off without a support burden?" Excited by leverage; wary of lock-in and per-client rebuilds.

## Jobs-to-be-done
- Build an automation for a client, then reuse it across projects (templates/recipes).
- Hand off something a non-technical client can run without her.

## What good looks like
A reusable template/recipe she builds once, deploys per client, and that the client can operate — with her able to manage multiple projects.

## Pet peeves
- Per-client rebuilds with no reuse. Lock-in she can't export out of.
- Anything that creates an ongoing support burden after handoff.

## Motivation — why use the app at all (time-saved)
- **Current/manual way:** bespoke n8n build per client, ~1–2 days each.
- **App should save:** build-once-reuse leverage + cleaner client handoff.

## Senior-quality bar (the reliability floor)
Deliverables a senior automation consultant would put their name on — robust, documented, client-operable.

## Surface binding (what THEY actually reach)
- Sections: Personas, Templates + Gallery (sharing), Teams, Cloud, Keys.
- Reaches builder/dev surfaces.

## Scored acceptance criteria (applied IDENTICALLY every run)
1. [missing/completion] She can turn a built automation into a reusable template/recipe.
2. [trust/portability] She can export/hand off without lock-in; client can operate it.
3. [time-saved] Second client is materially faster than the first (reuse leverage).
4. [senior-quality] The delivered automation is robust enough to bill for.
5. [clarity] Managing multiple client projects is legible, not a tangle.
