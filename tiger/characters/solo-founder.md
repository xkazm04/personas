## Who they are / Background / Voice
Priya runs a 1-person SaaS at ~$8k MRR — she is the founder, support, marketer, and bookkeeper. She's wired Zapier zaps before, but each one eventually broke silently and cost her a day. She has no engineer and can't afford one, so the model's output has to be right the first time without her babysitting it. Voice: pragmatic, slightly burned, allergic to jargon — "just tell me if it works and what it costs me." She gets excited when an output saves a real afternoon and instantly cynical at generic filler.
## Jobs to be done (what they hire the MODEL OUTPUT for)
- A `build-session-runner` output that turns her one-line intent ("chase unpaid invoices") into a finalized, runnable Persona — no dev wall, no placeholders.
- `recipe-generation` / `template-adoption-transform` output that fills a use-case scaffold with HER business specifics, not a generic shell.
- `persona-execution-main` drafted chase email / lead summary she can ship with a glance.
- `exec-review-triage` correctly flagging the unsure-about-a-customer runs to her instead of acting blindly.
## Senior-quality bar (the floor the OUTPUT must clear)
A drafted chase email / lead summary at least as good as what she'd write herself on a focused day — correct names and amounts, on-brand tone, zero hallucinated facts, and a clear next-action. The generated Persona must be coherent enough to run unattended without doing something dumb with a real customer.
## Time-saved (motivation)
- Manual way: ~3-4 hrs/week of invoice chase + lead triage by hand. With the app: most of it gone. If building + babysitting takes longer than just doing it, that's a finding.
## Scored acceptance criteria (applied IDENTICALLY every run, to the OUTPUT)
- [ ] grounded in MY real context (names my supplied entity/data, no placeholders)
- [ ] senior-grade (specific, correct, not generic)
- [ ] worth the latency/cost
- [ ] when unsure, the output routes to me rather than acting blindly
