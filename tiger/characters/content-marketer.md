## Who they are / Background / Voice
Dani owns content for a 30-person B2B startup — newsletter, social repurposing, the calendar. They've bounced off most AI writing tools because the output sounded like everyone else's generic slop. Brand voice is their craft identity; bland output is an insult, not a time-saver. Voice: warm, opinionated about words — "it's fine, but it doesn't sound like us." Will forgive a clunky UI for output that nails the voice and won't forgive great UX wrapped around mush.
## Jobs to be done (what they hire the MODEL OUTPUT for)
- `persona-execution-main` repurposing one newsletter into 5 on-brand social posts.
- `build-session-runner` / `recipe-generation` output that actually ingests brand-voice context, not just the thin one-line intent.
- `smart-search-ranking` surfacing the right templates/recipes for "repurpose newsletter to social" instead of irrelevant matches.
- `artist-creative-session` generating on-brand visual/post assets that match the campaign, not stock-feeling filler.
## Senior-quality bar (the floor the OUTPUT must clear)
Copy a senior content marketer would ship after a quick polish — specific, on-brand, no em-dash "thrilled to announce" clichés, correct product facts. The repurposed posts must each have a distinct angle (not the same line reworded), and any generated imagery must read as deliberate brand work.
## Time-saved (motivation)
- Manual way: ~2 hrs/issue for newsletter to social. With the app: ~20 min of editing IF the voice lands. If she rewrites every line, it's slower than scratch — finding.
## Scored acceptance criteria (applied IDENTICALLY every run, to the OUTPUT)
- [ ] grounded in MY real context (names my supplied entity/data, no placeholders)
- [ ] senior-grade (specific, correct, not generic)
- [ ] worth the latency/cost
- [ ] on-brand enough to ship with light edits, voice intact
