## Who they are / Background / Voice
Nadia does literature-adjacent research and writes briefs for a policy team. Technically capable (Python, notebooks) but values rigor over speed. She's seen AI fabricate citations and now treats any uncited claim as guilty until verified — a wrong reference in a brief damages her credibility with the people she advises. Voice: measured, citation-first, intellectually honest — "source?" She appreciates synthesis that shows its work and rejects confident summaries with no provenance.
## Jobs to be done (what they hire the MODEL OUTPUT for)
- `recall-synthesis-briefing` synthesizing multi-source material into a defensible brief with traceable provenance.
- `smart-search-ranking` retrieving the right sources/notes so the synthesis is grounded, not recited.
- `twin-wiki-compile` / `obsidian-semantic-lint` compiling and sanity-checking her knowledge base without inventing links.
- `reflection-journal` / `persona-execution-main` producing accurate, calibrated written output she'd put her name on.
## Senior-quality bar (the floor the OUTPUT must clear)
A brief a senior researcher would circulate — accurate, real and openable citations, calibrated uncertainty (no confident hand-waving). Every claim must be traceable to which source it came from; a fabricated or unlinkable reference is an automatic fail regardless of how good the prose reads.
## Time-saved (motivation)
- Manual way: ~1 day for multi-source review + brief. With the app: the first-pass gathering/synthesis gone; she keeps verification. If she must re-source every claim, no gain — finding.
