# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in the Personas Desktop project a welcoming and respectful experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standard

This project has adopted the **[Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)** as its code of conduct. The full text — including examples of positive behavior, unacceptable behavior, enforcement responsibilities, and enforcement guidelines — is maintained by the Contributor Covenant project and is incorporated here by reference.

Please read the full document at:
**https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/**

By participating in this project (opening issues, submitting pull requests, commenting in discussions, etc.), you agree to abide by its terms.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces maintained by the project — including the GitHub repository, issue tracker, pull requests, discussions, and any official communication channels — and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported privately to the project maintainers at:

**michal.kazdan@borndigital.ai**

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Project maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of anyone who reports an incident.

Enforcement actions follow the **Community Impact Guidelines** published as part of Contributor Covenant 2.1, which define a progression from private warnings, to temporary bans, to permanent bans, depending on the severity and repetition of the behavior.
