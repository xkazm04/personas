# Ledgerline — Design Brief (hand-written, for paste-verbatim test)

## 1. Empathy
Ledgerline serves solo founders and small-business owners who dread bookkeeping.
They are time-poor, finance-anxious, and distrust black-box automation. The brand
must feel like a calm, competent accountant friend — never a cold enterprise SaaS.
Emotional target: relief, quiet confidence, "I've got this handled for you."

## 2. Sensory Aesthetic
Warm-but-precise. Paper-ledger heritage meets modern fintech clarity. Soft matte
surfaces, hairline rules reminiscent of accounting paper, generous whitespace.
Tactile, never glossy. Motion is slow and reassuring (200–300ms ease-out), never
bouncy.

## 3. Color Hierarchy (four roles)
- **Primary (trust/action):** Ink Teal `#0E5C57` — buttons, key figures, totals.
- **Secondary (structure):** Ledger Paper `#F7F4EC` — backgrounds, cards.
- **Accent (positive signal):** Statement Green `#2FA866` — reconciled / in-the-black.
- **Alert (attention):** Audit Amber `#D98A2B` — overdue, needs-review (never pure red;
  red reads as failure, the user already feels enough finance anxiety).

## 4. Typography Hierarchy
- **Display/Numerals:** a humanist tabular-figure serif (e.g. Tiempos / Source Serif)
  for monetary figures — figures must align in columns like a real ledger.
- **Body/UI:** a quiet grotesque sans (e.g. Inter) at comfortable 16px, 1.55 line-height.
- Hierarchy by weight + size, not color. Totals are the largest, heaviest element.

## 5. Layout Metaphor + Do's / Don'ts
Metaphor: a **clean accountant's ledger page** — left-aligned line items, right-aligned
figures, a single horizontal rule separating sections, totals anchored bottom-right.
- DO: align all currency right; use tabular figures; keep one primary action per screen.
- DO: disclose the "why" behind every automated categorization (trust through transparency).
- DON'T: use pure red, dense dashboards, gradients, drop shadows heavier than 1 elevation.
- DON'T: hide numbers behind charts — the figure is the hero, the chart is support.
